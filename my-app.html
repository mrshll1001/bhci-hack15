Who's Rights Are They Anyway?

When we looked over the United Nations Universal Declaration of Human Rights we noticed that, the wording was very androcentric (focuses on men), and very heteronormative (focuses on 'straight' people), and fails to represent at all any other points of the gender and sexuality spectrums. Whilst we think that this language was a product of the time it was written (1948), we also think that this language fails to represent the entire spectrum of humanity. So we wrote in a few changes. Choose a version of the Human Rights below to see the various articles and who, by way of representation in the language, has what rights.

This page is the result of the British HCI 2015 Hackfest Workshop and the joint effort of Connie Golsteijn, Matt Marshall, Sebastian Mellor, and Angelika Strohmayer.
